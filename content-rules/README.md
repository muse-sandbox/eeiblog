## Property map

EE / EEi content lives across three properties:

- **eeiblog.com** — this blog (Teaching Tips, tutorials, feature explainers).
- **essentialelementsinteractive.com** — the product platform (sign-up, log-in, the actual app).
- **halleonard.com/ee/** — the publisher catalog (where books are gotten).

Plus the help center: **support.essentialelementsinteractive.com**.

## Voice in one sentence

EE / EEi sits at the **most refined end** of the Muse Group voice spectrum: clear, plain, professional, helpful — with quiet confidence rather than rebel posture. K–12 educators and school administrators are the audience.

## CTA voice

Lead with "**get**", "explore", "start", "request" — soft, low-friction action verbs. Do **not** lead with "buy", "purchase", or "Start free trial". EEi comes with every Essential Elements book — the message is access through the book, not a separate transaction.

## How to use these rules

Pass `eei-rules.md`, `eei-abbreviations.md`, and `eei-communications-problems.md` to any LLM (or human writer) drafting EE / EEi content. Together they form the brief: how to write, what to call things, and what mistakes to avoid.
