# Essential Elements & EEi — communications problems (AI hallucinations & corrections)

> This file collects **factual mistakes**, **AI hallucinations**, and **terminology drift** that have appeared in copy about Essential Elements (EE) and Essential Elements Interactive (EEi). Every entry is a defect we have to actively prevent in future drafts.
>
> Pattern: **Wrong claim → Why it's wrong → Correct phrasing.**
>
> Add new entries as they're caught in review. The bigger this list grows, the easier it is to spot the same mistake creeping back in.

---

## Pricing & access

### ❌ "Sign up for a free EEi trial."
**Why it's wrong:** EEi is not a stand-alone subscription product with a trial. It is included free with the purchase of any Essential Elements student or teacher book. (A teacher *can* request a separate teacher trial account on the platform, but that's not the message we lead with in blog copy.)
**✅ Correct:** "EEi is included free with every Essential Elements student and teacher book — no separate subscription."

### ❌ "Subscribe to EEi for $X per month."
**Why it's wrong:** No subscription pricing exists for student usage. Access is unlocked by the activation code printed inside each EE student book.
**✅ Correct:** "Each Essential Elements book includes an EEi activation code on the title page. Students enter that code to activate their EEi account."

### ❌ "EEi is a paid premium platform."
**Why it's wrong:** Premium positioning misrepresents the model. EEi is a value-added companion to a book the teacher already has.
**✅ Correct:** "EEi is the cloud-based companion to the Essential Elements method, included at no extra cost with every EE book."

### ❌ "Buy EEi access today."
**Why it's wrong:** EEi is not sold separately. Customers get an EE book and EEi comes with it. CTA voice should also avoid "buy" — lead with "get" / "explore" / "request".
**✅ Correct:** "Get the Essential Elements book your students need — EEi is included free."

## Product naming & positioning

### ❌ "EE Interactive" (with space)
**Why it's wrong:** The product name is **Essential Elements Interactive**, abbreviated **EEi** (no space, lowercase i).
**✅ Correct:** Use **Essential Elements Interactive** on first reference, **EEi** afterwards.

### ❌ "Hal Leonard's EEi app" / "EEi by Muse Group"
**Why it's wrong:** EEi is a Hal Leonard product. Hal Leonard is part of Muse Group, but EEi should be branded under Hal Leonard, with the Muse Group connection mentioned secondarily ("Hal Leonard, a Muse Group company").
**✅ Correct:** "Essential Elements Interactive (EEi), made by Hal Leonard, a Muse Group company."

### ❌ "EEi competes with Yousician / Smartmusic."
**Why it's wrong:** Per the writing rules, mention competitors at most twice and never lead with positioning. Smartmusic is the closest analog but EEi's value prop is bundled-with-method, not standalone practice tool.
**✅ Correct:** Lead with EEi's actual differentiator (built into the Essential Elements method, no extra cost, Music Studio + SoundCheck + assignments) and only reference competitors when comparison is the explicit topic.

## SoundCheck

### ❌ "SoundCheck uses AI to grade student performance."
**Why it's wrong:** SoundCheck is **powered by MatchMySound** — a third-party assessment engine. Calling it "AI" is reductive and invites questions we can't answer cleanly.
**✅ Correct:** "SoundCheck Performance Assessment, powered by MatchMySound, evaluates pitch and rhythm against the printed exercise."

### ❌ "SoundCheck gives every student a grade automatically."
**Why it's wrong:** SoundCheck provides assessment scores; the **teacher** decides whether scores become grades. Auto-grading framing implies the platform replaces teacher judgment.
**✅ Correct:** "SoundCheck gives students immediate pitch and rhythm feedback. Teachers choose how to use those scores in the gradebook."

## Music Studio

### ❌ "EEi Music Studio is a DAW for kids."
**Why it's wrong:** Music Studio is a practice and play-along tool, not a Digital Audio Workstation. There is no multi-track recording / production workflow.
**✅ Correct:** "The Music Studio is EEi's practice tool — students play along with professionally recorded accompaniments, slow them down, and record their performance."

### ❌ "Students can record original songs in Music Studio."
**Why it's wrong:** Music Studio is built around the EE method exercises and Bonus Songs library. It is not an open composition tool.
**✅ Correct:** "Students record themselves performing exercises and songs from their Essential Elements book."

## Accounts & accessing EEi

### ❌ "Sign in via the eeiblog." / "Create an EEi account on Hal Leonard's website."
**Why it's wrong:** Student and teacher accounts are created and accessed at **essentialelementsinteractive.com**, not on the blog (eeiblog.com) and not on the catalog (halleonard.com/ee/).
**✅ Correct:** "Sign in or create an account at [essentialelementsinteractive.com](https://www.essentialelementsinteractive.com/)."

### ❌ "Teachers must purchase a separate EEi license for their school."
**Why it's wrong:** No separate license. The school's book purchases unlock student access. Teacher accounts are free to create.
**✅ Correct:** "Teacher accounts are free. Each Essential Elements book purchased for the program unlocks an EEi student account."

### ❌ "EEi works on iPads only." / "Students need a special app."
**Why it's wrong:** EEi runs in a browser on any modern device — desktop, Chromebook, tablet, iPad. There is no separate native app for students.
**✅ Correct:** "EEi runs in any modern web browser, on desktop, Chromebook, or tablet — no app install required."

## Privacy / COPPA / school data

### ❌ "EEi collects student data for analytics."
**Why it's wrong:** Misrepresents the privacy posture. Student data handling is governed by the [EEi COPPA Privacy Policy](https://www.essentialelementsinteractive.com/EEI_COPPA_Privacy_Policy_Current.pdf). Don't fabricate analytics claims.
**✅ Correct:** "EEi follows COPPA-compliant practices for student accounts. See the [EEi COPPA Privacy Policy](https://www.essentialelementsinteractive.com/EEI_COPPA_Privacy_Policy_Current.pdf) for details."

## Pedagogy claims (do not invent)

### ❌ "Studies show EEi students improve XX% faster."
**Why it's wrong:** No published efficacy study with this kind of headline number. Don't invent statistics.
**✅ Correct:** Use teacher testimonials with attribution, or describe the workflow ("daily practice + immediate SoundCheck feedback") without quantitative outcome claims.

### ❌ "EEi is research-backed pedagogy."
**Why it's wrong:** The Essential Elements method has decades of classroom adoption, but specific "research-backed" framing without a cited study reads like marketing fluff.
**✅ Correct:** "Essential Elements has been the standard band/strings method for [N] years, used in thousands of music programs across the country."  *(Confirm exact figure with Hal Leonard before publishing.)*

## Technology / integration

### ❌ "EEi integrates with every LMS."
**Why it's wrong:** Confirmed integration is **Google Classroom** (SSO + assignment sync). Don't claim coverage we don't have.
**✅ Correct:** "EEi integrates with Google Classroom, providing single sign-on and assignment sync. Students and teachers can use their Google accounts to log in to EEi."

### ❌ "Students can submit assignments via Spotify / Apple Music / TikTok."
**Why it's wrong:** Submission flows are inside EEi (audio recording, video recording, in-class grading). No third-party media-platform submission.
**✅ Correct:** "Students record audio or video assignments directly in EEi and submit to their teacher's gradebook."

## Geography / catalog scope

### ❌ "EEi is available worldwide."
**Why it's wrong:** EEi is available wherever Hal Leonard distributes the EE method — primarily the US, with reach via Hal Leonard Europe distribution channels. Verify scope before making global claims.
**✅ Correct:** "EEi is available with every Essential Elements book sold by Hal Leonard. Check with your local Hal Leonard distributor for availability outside the US."
